Model No. D.O.M.O-001

A beautifully preserved tabletop radio from the early 1950s, this piece carries the warmth of a time when families gathered around a single glowing dial. The walnut veneer cabinet is in remarkable condition, with only the softest patina that decades of careful ownership leaves behind.

The AM band still pulls in signal with surprising clarity. The original speaker cloth — a warm amber weave — is intact and undamaged. This is not a reproduction. This is the real thing.

The Story

Found in a Wisconsin Attic, 1952.

Every object in the Department of Miscellaneous Objects comes with a provenance — a thread that connects the present to the moment it was made. This radio was discovered in a cedar-lined attic outside Madison, Wisconsin, packed in its original cardboard box alongside a stack of TV Guide magazines from the same decade.

The previous owner — a retired schoolteacher named Ruth — used it every evening to listen to baseball games and big band broadcasts. She kept meticulous notes in a small green ledger: which stations had the clearest signal, which dial positions to avoid on rainy nights. Those notes come with the radio.

Objects like this don't show up often. When they do, we believe they deserve to be in the hands of someone who will use them — not just display them.
